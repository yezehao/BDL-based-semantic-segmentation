Bayesian Inference

Bayes' theorem

\[\begin{aligned} \small \text{posterior}=\frac{\text{likelihood} * \text{prior}}{\text{marginal likelihood}} \end{aligned} \]
### Example - Flip a Coin + The coin is bias, which means the $p(tail) â‰  0.5$; + Estimate based on common sense, $p(tail) = 0.5$; + The prior: $p(hypostesis) = 0.5$;
### Example - Flip a Coin + If flip the coin $n$ times and get $m$ tail; + Likelihood: $p(D|m, \lambda) = \frac{n!}{m!(n-m)!}\lambda^{m}\left(1-\lambda\right)^{n-m}$; + $\hat{\lambda}_{ML} = \underset{\lambda}{argmax}(p(D|m, \lambda)) = \frac{m}{n}$;
